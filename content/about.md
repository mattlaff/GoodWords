## about poetry

My favorite Good Words have always been poems and quotes. I have squirrelled away for myself many of both, and it's time I did something besides hoard them, so here we are.

I've loved poetry ever since I discovered Dr. Seuss and Shel Silverstein as a child, but adult me (even with an English degree in my back pocket) had not always been particularly intentional about regularly reading it... until the early days of 2020 (it was my sourdough), when I started not just reading more poetry but also manually typing out poems I enjoyed as I encountered them.

It quickly became one of the things that kept me afloat that year, but even as we began telling ourselves things were returning to normal (whatever that means), I kept right on copying poems. Anymore, I don't do it every day or even every week, but I still do it, so I'd like to invite you along as I go through the words I've saved and those I've yet to discover.

## about quotations

Similarly, I've collected quotes since I was in high school in the late 90s, because I have always loved the way a carefully distilled thought can nestle in the mind and heart like a gemstone into its setting. The quotes I've gathered over the years have helped shape the way I see and move through the world.

The quotes I've saved have helped me understand the worlds inside me and out, and again, I'd like to invite you to explore them with me.

## praxis

Sharing poems can be a tricky thing, because obviously I don't have the rights to anyone else's work. My plan, then, is to share excerpts and link out as responsibly as possible unless I genuinely cannot find the complete poem at all anymore.

Sharing quotes is safer, though a quick word about categorization might be in order. I've tinkered with various methods over the years, but for my own sanity I've grown fond of keeping things as simple as possible, and to that end I now have just five categories:

* **Beauty:** _beauty and inspiration of all kinds, as well as the creative process itself_
* **Love:** _"relationships of all kinds, hospitality, shared experience, and what we owe each other_
* **Mystery:** _the limits of human perception and language, awe and wonder, and God (whatever that means)_
* **Presence:** _staying rooted in the moment, life as practice, bridging the gap between thinking and doing, the value of struggle, becoming_
* **Truth:** _the unending search search for the structure of reality, including the work of examining biases and diagnosing error_

Naturally, there is overlap to be found among those categories, but I've also grown fond of the blur. Anyway, we all know no system is perfect or perfectly implemented, so I categorize as best I can in the moment and leave it at that.
