My favorite Good Words have always been poems and quotes, and I have squirrelled away for myself many of both, but I want to do something other than hoard them, so here we are.

I've loved poetry ever since I discovered Dr. Seuss and Shel Silverstein as a child, but adult me (even with an English degree) had not always been particularly intentional about regularly reading it until the early days of 2020 (_I know, I know_), when I started a practice of manually copying (well, typing out) poems I enjoyed.

It quickly became one of the things that kept me afloat those days, but even as we began telling ourselves things were returning to normal, I kept copying poems. I don't do it every day or even every week, but I now have a series of folders and documents filled with poems I'd like to share with anyone would like to read them.

Similarly, I've collected quotes since I was in high school in the late 90s, and it's not an overstatement to say that the quotes I've gathered over the years have helped shape the way I see and move through the world.

All of these poems and quotes have done and continue to do me good, but as I've said, it's feeling increasingly silly to have them all gathering digital cobwebs, so here we are.

## praxis

Sharing poems is a tricky thing. I don't have the rights to anyone else's poems, so I plan on sharing excerpts and linking out as responsibly as possible unless I can't find the complete poem at all anymore.

Sharing quotes feels easier, but a quick word about categorization might be in order. I've tinkered with various methods over the years, but I've grown fond of keeping things as simple as possible, and to that end I now have just five categories:

* **Beauty:** _"Does this celebrate natural or created beauty, extoll the value of inspiration and self-expression, or provide insight into the creative process?"_
* **Love:** _"Does this help me understand how we should relate to each other if our aim is shared flourishing?"_
* **Mystery:** _"Does this point toward that which lies beyond our comprehension?"_
* **Presence:** _"Does this help me understand how to be in the now, even/especially when the being is difficult?"_
* **Truth:** _"Does this favor epistemic humility, systemic integrity, and the elevation of data over dogma?"_

Obviously, there is some overlap to be found among the categories, but we all know no system is perfect or perfectly implemented, so I categorize as it seems to make sense to me at the time and leave it at that.
